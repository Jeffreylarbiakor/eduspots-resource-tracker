01

What this tool is

A single, network-wide view of how books, tablets, and other learning materials are distributed across EduSpots' Spots — and where the gaps are.

Right now, distribution tracking across 50+ Spots likely lives in a patchwork of spreadsheets, WhatsApp messages, and local knowledge held by individual Regional Coordinators. That works when the network is small. It starts to break down when you need to answer questions like: which Spots haven't received a book delivery in six months? Which requests have been sitting open for 45 days? Is the Northern cluster consistently under-resourced compared to Volta?

This tracker consolidates that picture. It doesn't replace the spreadsheets or the RC relationships — it sits above them, giving the Head of Programme and Product Operations a network-level view that makes distribution decisions faster, fairer, and easier to justify to funders.

Network-wide in one view
See all 49 Spots and their resourcing status without opening a single spreadsheet or asking an RC for an update.
Specific, actionable flags
Every flagged Spot carries the exact reason — "0 devices, 52 Sparks reached" not just a red dot — so you know what action is needed before you open the detail.
Request queue, not a black box
Every resource request — from date raised to days open to fulfilment — is visible in one table, sorted and filtered however you need it.
Human decision, always
The tool surfaces what to notice. Every allocation, follow-up, and resourcing call is made by a person — an RC, the Head of Ops, or both together.

02

The core principle

Automation surfaces what to notice. A human decides what to do about it.

🌿
Why this matters for distribution decisions

A tool that auto-allocates resources to flagged Spots sounds efficient, but it removes the judgment that makes allocation fair. An RC knows that Bimbilla has a temporary closure, that a Book Aid International shipment is already en route to Katanga-Zuarungu, that Savelugu's RC has a follow-up call scheduled for next week. The tracker doesn't have that context. So it flags; you decide.

This principle is built into every interactive element in the tracker. The "Acknowledge for follow-up" button on a flagged Spot doesn't raise a request or queue a delivery — it records that a human has eyes on it. The request table doesn't auto-close requests when they age past a threshold — it shows you exactly how old they are and lets you act. The status rules are written out plainly in the README and in this document so there are no surprises about why something was flagged.

03

Overview view

The starting screen. A pulse-check on the whole network before you go deeper.

Overview
KPI row · 12-month trend chart · cluster distribution chart

The Overview loads first when you open the tracker. It answers the most common Monday-morning question: what's the state of distribution across the network right now?

The KPI row gives five numbers at a glance. The two charts underneath show whether distribution is trending in the right direction over time, and whether any RC cluster is being systematically under-served.

  • Total books distributed Cumulative estimate of books on hand across all Spots. Useful as a headline figure for funder reports or board updates. In a live system, this would reflect confirmed distribution records rather than estimates.
  • Total devices distributed Tablets and other devices on hand across the network. Devices are the scarcest resource in the EduSpots model, so this number warrants particular attention when it's low relative to Sparks reached.
  • Open / unfulfilled requests Any request that hasn't been fulfilled yet — includes both "open" (no action taken) and "in progress" (being worked). A rising number here is a signal that fulfilment capacity or partner supply is being stretched.
  • Spots below benchmark The count of Spots flagged as either "below benchmark" or "under-resourced" by the status rules. This is the number to track week-on-week: going up means gaps are widening; going down means follow-ups are working.
  • Avg. request-to-fulfilment time How long it takes on average from a Spot raising a request to that request being marked fulfilled. Useful for capacity planning and for conversations with partners about lead times.
  • 12-month trend chart A stacked bar chart showing books, tablets, and other materials distributed each month for the past year. Lets you spot seasonal patterns (e.g. large book shipments timed to school terms) and identify months where distribution dropped unexpectedly.
  • Resources by RC cluster A horizontal grouped bar comparing books and devices on hand across the four RC clusters: Northern Region, Volta Region, Central/Western Regions, and New Spots. An obvious imbalance here is a prompt for a conversation with that cluster's RC about whether distribution has been reaching all their Spots.

04

Spot resourcing grid

All 49 Spots as colour-coded tiles. The fastest way to see which Spots need attention.

Spot resourcing grid
49 tiles · filterable by status and RC cluster · click for detail

The grid gives you a spatial scan of the network. Green-bordered tiles are fine. Gold means worth watching. Clay/red means something specific has been flagged and needs follow-up. You can see the whole picture in seconds without reading a single row of a spreadsheet.

Filter by status to isolate only the Spots that need attention. Filter by RC cluster to prepare for a check-in call with a specific coordinator — you'll see exactly which of their Spots are flagged before you dial in.

Clicking any tile opens a detail panel for that Spot: books and devices on hand, last distribution date, Sparks reached, and all requests linked to that Spot (open, in progress, and fulfilled).

  • Tile colour Green border = well-resourced. Gold border = below benchmark. Clay/red border = under-resourced. The colour encodes the computed status — no manual tagging required.
  • Tile content Each tile shows the Spot name, its RC cluster, a status badge, and a quick read of books on hand and devices on hand.
  • Status filter Narrow to "under-resourced" only to get a focused list for an urgent resourcing meeting, or "below benchmark" to see what's at risk of slipping further.
  • Cluster filter Shows only Spots within a specific RC's cluster — useful for preparing cluster-level reporting or individual RC check-ins.
  • Spot detail modal Click any tile to see full detail: books on hand (estimate), devices on hand, Sparks reached, last distribution date, and the complete request history for that Spot. Close with ✕ or press Escape.

05

Requests & fulfilment

The operational heart of the tracker — a lightweight ticket queue for every resource request across the network.

Requests & fulfilment
Sortable table · filterable by status and type · click any row for detail

Every resource request in the network lives in this table. Think of it as the operational to-do list for distribution: what has been asked for, by which Spot, how long ago, and where it sits in the fulfilment process.

Sorting by "Days open" descending surfaces the oldest unresolved requests first — the ones most likely to be causing a resourcing problem at a Spot. Filtering by "Open" status narrows to requests that haven't been acted on at all yet.

Clicking any row opens the request detail panel, which includes full request information, which Spot and RC cluster it's tied to, and a source or partner note where one exists (e.g. "Partner donation — Book Aid International").

  • Request ID A unique reference (e.g. REQ-014) for each request. In a live system, this would be the reference used in partner correspondence and internal handoffs.
  • Resource type Books, Tablets, or Other materials. Filter by type to see, for example, how many tablet requests are currently open — useful when preparing for a partner conversation about device supply.
  • Days open How many days have elapsed since the request was raised, for all non-fulfilled requests. This is the key column to watch: requests past 30 days affect a Spot's "below benchmark" status; past 60 days push a Spot to "under-resourced."
  • Status badges Open — no action taken. In progress — being worked or partially fulfilled. Fulfilled — fulfilment date logged.
  • Column sorting Click any column header to sort. Click again to reverse. Useful combinations: sort by Spot name to group requests by Spot; sort by Date requested to see the oldest requests; sort by Status to see all open requests together.
  • Source / partner note Visible in the request detail panel. Where a source is noted (e.g. "Partner donation — Book Aid International"), it links the request to a specific supply route — helpful for tracking which partner relationships are being utilised.

06

Under-resourced Spots

The action view — every Spot that has crossed a threshold, with the specific reason and a way to take ownership of the follow-up.

Under-resourced Spots
Flagged Spots only · specific reasons · acknowledge for follow-up

This view collects every Spot that has failed at least one resourcing threshold — sorted with the most serious cases (under-resourced) at the top. Each card shows not just the status, but exactly why the Spot was flagged, so you arrive at a follow-up conversation already knowing what the problem is.

The "Acknowledge for follow-up" button records that someone — an RC, the Head of Ops, or HQ — has taken ownership of reviewing that Spot's situation. It doesn't raise a request, it doesn't send a notification, and it doesn't fulfil an allocation. It's a human handshake: I've seen this, I'm on it.

  • Specific flagged reasons Each card lists the exact condition(s) that triggered the flag, e.g. "No distribution logged in 10 months" or "0 devices on hand — 47 Sparks reached" or "2 open requests older than 60 days." Never just a colour.
  • Spot context on the card Books on hand, devices on hand, Sparks reached, and last distribution date are shown directly on the card — you don't need to open a detail panel to understand the situation.
  • Acknowledge for follow-up Marks a Spot as having been reviewed. The button becomes a green "✓ Acknowledged" badge. In this prototype this resets on refresh; in a live system it would persist with a timestamp and the name of whoever acknowledged it.
  • View Spot detail Opens the full Spot detail modal — same as clicking the tile in the grid — so you can see the request history without switching views.
  • Sort order Under-resourced Spots always appear above below-benchmark Spots, so the most urgent cases are at the top of the list without any manual sorting.
💡
How to use this view in a weekly ops review

Open this view at the start of each week. Work through the under-resourced cards first. For each one: does the flagged reason match something you're already aware of? If yes, acknowledge it. If no, it needs a call with the relevant RC. Then move to below-benchmark — these are the ones to watch before they tip into under-resourced.

07

How status is calculated

Every status is computed by a named function with explicit rules — no hidden scoring, no black box.

The rules are the same regardless of which view you're in. A Spot flagged as "under-resourced" on the grid is flagged for the same reason it appears in the Under-resourced Spots view. The reason is always shown alongside the status.

Spot resourcing status

Under-resourced
Any one of the following:
No distribution logged in 9+ months, OR devices on hand is 0 while the Spot has 40 or more Sparks reached, OR 2 or more open requests older than 60 days.
Below benchmark
Any one of the following (and not already under-resourced):
No distribution logged in 4–8 months, OR 1 or more open requests older than 30 days.
Well-resourced
None of the above conditions apply.
Distribution is within the last 4 months and there are no stale open requests.

Request status

Open
No fulfilment action has been logged yet. The request is sitting unaddressed.
In progress
Partially fulfilled, or explicitly flagged as being actively worked — for example, a delivery is confirmed but not yet received.
Fulfilled
A fulfilment date has been logged. The request is considered closed.
📋
The thresholds can be adjusted

The 9-month, 4-month, 30-day, and 60-day thresholds in the status rules are starting points — not fixed policy. In a live deployment, the Head of Ops and RCs would review these together and set thresholds that reflect what EduSpots considers a healthy distribution cadence for each resource type.

08

Typical weekly workflow

How the Head of Programme and Product Operations would use this in practice — from Monday morning to a distribution decision.

1
Open Overview — read the pulse
Check the KPI row. Has the number of Spots below benchmark gone up or down since last week? Is the open request count climbing? Is average fulfilment time trending longer? These numbers set the agenda for the rest of the session.
2
Go to Under-resourced Spots — triage the urgent cases
Work through the under-resourced cards first. For each: is this already known and in hand? Acknowledge it. Is it a surprise? Note it for an RC call. Repeat for below-benchmark cards — these are the early warnings before a situation becomes urgent.
3
Go to Requests & Fulfilment — sort by days open
Sort the table by "Days open" descending. Any request past 60 days that's still open is directly affecting a Spot's resourcing status. Identify which ones need a fulfilment push — a partner chase, a purchase order, or a reallocation from a surplus Spot.
4
Prepare for RC cluster calls — filter the grid by cluster
Before a check-in call with, say, Cynthia Mawuena Tetteh (Volta Region), filter the Spot grid to Volta Region. See at a glance which of her Spots are flagged, click through to the detail for specific ones, and arrive at the call with a focused agenda rather than a general "how's it going?"
5
Make the allocation decision — outside the tool
The tracker tells you what's needed and where. The actual allocation — who gets the next Book Aid International shipment, which Spot gets priority for the next tablet purchase — is made by the Head of Ops and the relevant RC together, using the tracker as the shared evidence base.

Who does what

The tracker serves three roles differently. The tool does the monitoring; people make the decisions.

Task Who In the tool
Monitor network-wide resourcing status Head of Ops Overview KPIs + Under-resourced Spots view
Identify which Spots need an urgent intervention Head of Ops Under-resourced Spots — clay/red cards
Prepare for cluster check-in calls Head of Ops RC Spot grid filtered by RC cluster
Chase stale or overdue requests Head of Ops RC Requests table sorted by days open
Record that a flagged Spot is being reviewed RC Head of Ops Acknowledge for follow-up button
Decide which Spot receives a resource allocation Head of Ops RC Outside the tool — informed by it
Flag distribution trends for funder reports Head of Ops Overview charts · 12-month trend

10

Next steps if built for real

This is a working prototype built on synthetic data. Here's what a live deployment would add.

Live data connection
Connect to a shared Google Sheet or Airtable base that RCs already update — so the tracker reflects real distribution records without requiring a new data entry habit.
Email / WhatsApp alerts
Automated weekly digest to the Head of Ops and each RC listing new flags in their cluster — so the tracker pushes urgent information rather than waiting to be opened.
CSV export
Export the requests table or the flagged Spots list to CSV for inclusion in RC cluster reports, partner updates, or board packs — without reformatting anything manually.
Per-Spot distribution history
A timeline view inside each Spot's detail panel showing every distribution event — useful for identifying patterns like Spots that receive large one-off shipments but no follow-up.
Integration with Project #1
Cross-reference resourcing status with Spot health data from the MEL Dashboard — so a Spot with low activity AND low resourcing surfaces as a combined priority rather than two separate flags.
Acknowledge with attribution
Persist acknowledgements with a timestamp and the name of the person who logged them — so there's a clear audit trail of who took ownership of a flagged Spot and when.
